Gift Gardens

Growing Food, Growing Community

Community gardens maintained by youth and adult volunteers. Proceeds are distributed by partner EPIC-CURE to combat food insecurity in St. Augustine.

Volunteer in the Gardens

How It Works

Neighbors cultivate abundance together.

CSA brings volunteers, garden leaders, families, and local partners together to plant, tend, harvest, and share fresh food. Gardens become outdoor classrooms where young people learn stewardship and adults build lasting friendships.

  • Volunteers prepare beds, plant seasonal produce, and maintain shared garden spaces.
  • Harvests and proceeds support local food security efforts through EPIC-CURE.
  • Each garden site welcomes newcomers, experienced growers, and families.

Volunteer

Bring your hands, your heart, and a little sun protection.

Garden days are welcoming, practical, and rooted in service. No gardening experience is required; team leaders help match volunteers with accessible, meaningful tasks.

Partner: EPIC-CURE

Connecting fresh food to families.

EPIC-CURE helps ensure the generosity grown in CSA gardens reaches people facing food insecurity. Together, the partnership strengthens the path from seed to table across St. Augustine.

Shared goal: increase access to fresh produce while building a volunteer culture that treats food security as a community responsibility.
