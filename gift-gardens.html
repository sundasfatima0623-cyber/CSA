Gift Gardens

Growing Food, Growing Community

Gift Gardens are community gardens maintained by youth and adult volunteers. Harvests are shared with partner EPIC-CURE to help combat food insecurity in St. Augustine, turning local care into practical nourishment for neighbors.

Volunteer in the Gardens

How It Works

From shared labor to shared abundance

CSA organizes planting days, seasonal maintenance, and harvesting with volunteers of many ages. Produce is cultivated with care, then directed through community partnerships so fresh food can reach households who need it most.

The gardens also serve as places of learning where residents can practice stewardship, gain practical skills, and deepen relationships across neighborhoods.

Volunteer

Ways to lend your hands

  • Planting and upkeep Help prepare beds, sow seeds, water, weed, and maintain healthy gardens.
  • Youth mentorship Encourage young volunteers through hands-on learning and teamwork.
  • Harvest and delivery support Assist with gathering produce and preparing it for partner distribution.

Partner: EPIC-CURE

Harvest shared with purpose

EPIC-CURE helps connect the abundance grown in Gift Gardens to people facing food insecurity. Through this partnership, each harvest becomes part of a broader network of dignity, care, and community nourishment in St. Augustine.

Current Garden Locations

Gardens rooted across the city

  • Davis Shores Demonstration Beds Raised beds focused on easy-to-grow produce and volunteer training.
