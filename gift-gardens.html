Growing Food, Growing Community

Gift Gardens are community gardens nurtured by youth and adult volunteers who believe every family should have access to fresh food.

Volunteer in the Gardens

How It Works

Volunteers prepare, plant, and maintain neighborhood garden beds throughout the season. Each harvest is sorted and distributed through our partner, EPIC-CURE, to combat food insecurity across St. Augustine.

From planning days to harvest celebrations, Gift Gardens creates meaningful intergenerational connections while growing healthy, local produce.

Volunteer

No gardening experience is required. We welcome all ages and skill levels for planting days, weekly care teams, and produce sorting support.

  • Weekly Garden Teams
  • Youth Service Opportunities
  • Seasonal Planting Days
  • Harvest & Distribution Support

Current Garden Locations

West Augustine Garden

MLK Avenue corridor hub for family and youth volunteer days.

Lincolnville Community Beds

Raised beds serving nearby neighbors and apartment residents.

South City Patch

Compact urban plots focusing on herbs and seasonal greens.

North Davis Teaching Garden

Education-focused space for composting and youth gardening lessons.

Partner: EPIC-CURE

EPIC-CURE distributes produce from Gift Gardens to neighbors experiencing food insecurity. This partnership helps us move from harvest to households quickly and responsibly.

Together, we are reducing hunger and building a stronger local network of care across St. Augustine.
