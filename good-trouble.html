What Is Good Trouble?

Advocacy Rooted in Compassion

Good Trouble is CSA's advocacy initiative — named in honor of the late Congressman John Lewis, who called on all of us to "get in good trouble, necessary trouble" in the pursuit of justice.

We build coalitions with local leaders, organizations, faith communities, and politicians to address St. Augustine's most pressing challenges through grassroots mobilization, compassionate dialogue, and strategic action.

Good Trouble is not protest for its own sake. It is organized, compassionate, and purposeful advocacy — rooted in our city's history as a birthplace of the civil rights movement.

Where We Focus

Our Action Areas

Good Trouble concentrates its energy where compassionate advocacy is needed most — in the pressing crises affecting St. Augustine's most vulnerable residents.

🏠

Affordable Housing

St. Augustine's housing costs are squeezing out longtime residents and working families. We advocate for policies, zoning reforms, and investments that keep this city home for everyone — not just the wealthy.

🏕️

Homelessness Crisis

Homelessness is a community failure and a compassionate community responds with dignity, services, and solutions. We work with service providers, city officials, and neighbors to address the root causes.

🌿

Environment

St. Augustine sits at sea level on a historic coastline. We advocate for climate resilience, environmental justice, and policies that protect our fragile ecosystems and the communities most at risk.
