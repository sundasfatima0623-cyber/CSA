Aging with Dignity, Surrounded by Community

Helping older adults in St. Augustine stay safe, connected, and joyfully at home — together.

Our Mission

Keeping seniors connected, independent, and cared for

CASA is CSA’s newest initiative — a community-rooted response to a quiet truth in St. Augustine: many older neighbors want to remain in their own homes but need a little help to do so safely and joyfully.

We weave together volunteer support, neighborly connection, and county partnerships so that no senior in our community has to navigate aging alone.

How We Help

Small acts. Life-changing impact.

🏡

Volunteer Home Visits

Friendly visits, light help around the house, and a familiar face to look forward to.

🚗

Transportation Assistance

Rides to medical appointments, the grocery store, and community gatherings.

🌷

Community Connection

Phone check-ins, social gatherings, and neighborhood pairings that build real friendship.

🏛️

County Coordination

Helping seniors and families navigate St. Johns County resources and services.

For Seniors

Asking for help is a kind thing to do

If you — or someone you love — could use a little extra support to keep enjoying life at home, we would be honored to walk alongside you. There is no cost, no obligation, and no judgment.

  • A friendly volunteer companion
  • Help with errands or transportation
  • Connection to local resources and services

For Volunteers

Give your time — receive so much in return

CASA volunteers are neighbors of every age and background, united by a simple belief: that our older residents deserve company, dignity, and a hand to hold. Even one visit a month makes a real difference.

  • Flexible scheduling — give what you can
  • Training and orientation provided
  • Background checks and ongoing support

County Partnership

Working with St. Johns County

CASA is partnering with St. Johns County to help fill the gaps in senior services — connecting existing programs with the on-the-ground volunteer support that makes them work for our oldest neighbors.

  • Service MappingIdentifying where county resources meet — and miss — real-world senior needs.
  • Referral PathwaysConnecting seniors and families directly to county case managers.
  • Volunteer BridgePairing CASA volunteers with county-supported individuals for ongoing care.
  • Community VoiceBringing the lived experiences of older adults to county planning conversations.
